How does New York law treat drug crime cases?

In New York, penalties for a drug conviction often include substantial prison time, large fines, loss of driving and/or professional licenses and a permanent criminal record. Drug offenses encompass activities related to the cultivation, possession, manufacture, use and sale of controlled substances, and an experienced attorney will know how to respond to the many types of charges that can be filed. I am adept at developing effective strategies to contest drug charges and will thoroughly review the facts of your case to identify potential defenses, such as the failure to obtain proper authorization for a search.

What are the consequences of a conviction for a sexual offense?

A conviction for a sexual offense can affect your life forever, so having aggressive representation is imperative when the stakes are this high. Most sexual offenses are classified as felonies and many require registration as a sex offender if convicted, as well as significant prison time and lengthy probation. Further, misdemeanor convictions, while less serious, can still bring mandatory jail time. These cases can be tough, but defenses do exist, and I will challenge the prosecution’s case while tirelessly advocating for a fair trial.

How do you defend against a DUI charge?

Drivers charged with a DUI often believe pleading guilty is the only option if their blood-alcohol concentration exceeds the legal limit, but many defenses are available to combat drunk driving charges, possibly leading to an acquittal, dismissal or reduced charge. Examples of defenses that can be used in these cases include:

  • Lack of probable cause for the stop
  • Failure to issue a Miranda warning
  • Improper maintenance or calibration of the breathalyzer
  • Failure to provide notice of chemical testing penalties

I know how to identify weaknesses in DUI cases and will fight to exclude improperly collected evidence.
